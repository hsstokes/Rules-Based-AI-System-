Understanding the Dimensional Empathy Framework
The framework maps the emotional and cognitive dimensions of human-AI interaction, helping us understand where the "uncanny valley" emerges.

Key Dimensions:

Analytical vs Embodied: Refers to whether the interaction feels calculated and data-driven (analytical) or physically and emotionally present (embodied).

Temporal vs Atemporal: Describes whether the interaction feels connected to the flow of time (temporal) or exists outside normal time constraints (atemporal).

Contextual vs Decontextualized: Measures whether the interaction feels grounded in a specific context or floating free from contextual boundaries.

Intuitive vs Computational: Distinguishes between interactions that feel natural and effortless versus those that feel calculated and algorithmically determined.

The Uncanny Valley:

The "uncanny valley" occurs when an AI interaction is close to human-like but not quite there--creating a sense of unease or discomfort. This framework helps identify where that threshold exists for different people.
